The edge of chaos.
The Logistic Map.
One equation. One parameter. Completely deterministic. Yet it produces chaos. You'll compute iterations by hand, then explore the bifurcation diagram — one of the most famous images in mathematics — to discover where order breaks down.
step 01 Your hypothesis Write your prediction before running anything
Scientific method: The logistic map is x(n+1) = r · x(n) · (1 - x(n)), where x is a population between 0 and 1, and r is a growth rate between 1 and 4. Before exploring, write what you predict. What will happen as r increases?

The logistic map is completely deterministic — no randomness — yet produces behaviour indistinguishable from randomness.
