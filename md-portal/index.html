MD PORTAL · TERSOFF MOLECULAR DYNAMICS
SiGe Surface Dynamics
Molecular dynamics simulation of silicon-germanium surfaces using the Tersoff interatomic potential (PRB 39, 5566, 1989). Build a diamond cubic (100) slab with dimer reconstruction, run velocity Verlet integration, and explore surface segregation through Monte Carlo species swaps. Real-time 3D visualization powered by Three.js.
Physics: The Tersoff potential captures both two-body repulsion and three-body bond-order effects. Each atom's bonding depends on its local coordination environment through an angular function g(θ). This allows the potential to distinguish between bulk diamond, surface dimers, and amorphous configurations — essential for studying surface reconstruction and SiGe intermixing.

CARD 01
Does Ge segregate to the surface?
Question: In a 50/50 SiGe alloy, does Ge preferentially occupy surface sites?
Method: Set θ=0.5, run T-sweep at 300, 500, 700, 900 K with MC swaps enabled. Track Ge fraction in the top layer. Look for: Ge enrichment at the surface that increases with temperature.
CARD 02
How does temperature affect the surface?
Question: Does the surface become more disordered at higher temperatures?
Method: Pure Si slab, T-sweep from 300 to 1200 K. Track energy/atom and watch 3D viewer. Look for: Energy increases with T. At high T, surface atoms vibrate more and dimers may break.
CARD 03
Composition and surface energy
Question: How does changing the Ge fraction affect the total energy?
Method: Sweep initial θ from 0 (pure Si) to 1 (pure Ge) at T=500 K. Look for: Energy per atom changes due to different Si-Si, Si-Ge, and Ge-Ge bond strengths. Vegard's law for the lattice constant.
