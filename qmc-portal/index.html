QMC PORTAL · QUANTUM MONTE CARLO
Particle in a Box
Variational and Diffusion Monte Carlo for the quantum particle-in-a-box problem. Compute ground-state and excited-state energies in 1D, 2D, and 3D. Optionally introduce fractal boundaries (Koch, Sierpinski) to explore how geometry changes quantum confinement. All energies in units of ℏ²/(2mL²).
Physics: A quantum particle confined in a box has discrete energy levels. In 1D: E_n = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²). Monte Carlo methods — Variational (VMC) and Diffusion (DMC) — find these energies numerically by sampling wavefunctions. With fractal boundaries, the eigenvalues shift in non-trivial ways governed by the fractal dimension.
