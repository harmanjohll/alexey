ARDUINO PORTAL · MOTOR CONTROL LAB
Motors, Servos & Control
Explore the physics of motor control with Arduino. Simulate DC motors responding to PWM signals, tune PID controllers in real time, and document your physical builds. Every motor obeys F = ma — the simulation uses the same Euler integration as orbital mechanics, just applied to torque and angular momentum.
Physics: A DC motor converts electrical energy to rotation via the Lorentz force. The motor equation V = IR + Keω links voltage, current, and speed. PWM controls the effective voltage. A PID controller adjusts the output to minimize error between desired and actual position — the same control theory used in rockets, robots, and self-driving cars.

CARD 01
Is motor speed proportional to PWM?
Question: If you double the PWM duty cycle, does the motor spin twice as fast?
Method: DC Motor tab. Set load torque to 0.01 N·m. Sweep duty cycle from 10% to 100% in steps of 10%. Record steady-state RPM at each step. Look for: Is the relationship linear? Is there a dead zone at low duty cycles where the motor doesn't spin?
CARD 02
Can you stop overshoot without losing speed?
Question: In a PID controller, can you eliminate overshoot while still reaching the target quickly?
Method: PID Tuner tab. Start with P=2.0, I=0, D=0. Increase P until you see oscillation. Then add D to dampen it. Then add I to remove steady-state error. Look for: The tradeoff between speed and stability. What happens when D is too high?
CARD 03
How does inertia change the game?
Question: If you double the load inertia (heavier arm), do you need to double the PID gains?
Method: PID Tuner tab. Find good PID gains for J=0.01. Then change to J=0.02 without changing gains. Look for: Does it oscillate? Undershoot? How must you adjust the gains to restore good tracking?

SERVOServo Control
How servos work: A servo receives a PWM signal with period ~20 ms. The pulse width (1000–2000 μs) maps to angle (0–180°). An internal PID controller drives the motor to match the target angle. You control position, not speed.
