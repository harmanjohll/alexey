How long is a coastline?
Fractal Dimension.
The coastline paradox: the length you measure depends on the size of your ruler. As the ruler shrinks, the measured length grows without bound. You'll generate fractal shapes, measure them with different ruler sizes, and discover that fractals have dimensions between 1 and 2.

Benoit Mandelbrot showed in 1967 that coastlines have fractal properties — their measured length depends on the ruler size. The Koch snowflake is a mathematical idealization, but real coastlines behave similarly. Britain's coastline has a fractal dimension of approximately 1.25.
