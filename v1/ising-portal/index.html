2D Ising Model
Metropolis Monte Carlo simulation of a 2D square lattice of spins. Explore the ferromagnetic phase transition at Tc ≈ 2.269 J/kB. Watch magnetic order emerge and collapse as you change temperature.
Physics: Each site carries a spin ±1. Nearest-neighbor coupling H = −J Σ sisj. At low T, spins align (ferromagnet). At Tc ≈ 2.269, thermal fluctuations destroy long-range order. This is the simplest model showing a continuous phase transition with universal critical exponents.

CARD 01
Where does magnetism disappear?
Question: At what temperature does the 2D Ising model lose its spontaneous magnetization?
Method: Sweep T from 1.0 to 4.0 (20 points), L=100, h=0, 500 sweeps/point. Look for: |M| drops sharply near T ≈ 2.27. Susceptibility χ and specific heat Cv peak at the same temperature.
CARD 02
Does size matter?
Question: How does the lattice size affect the sharpness of the phase transition?
Method: Run T-sweeps at L = 20, 50, 100, 200 and overlay the curves. Look for: Larger L → sharper transition. The Onsager exact solution (infinite L) is shown as a dashed line. All curves should converge toward it.
CARD 03
Can you magnetize a random system?
Question: Below Tc, does the system remember which direction it was magnetized?
Method: Fix T = 1.5 (well below Tc). Sweep external field h from +2 to −2 and back. Look for: A hysteresis loop. Remanent magnetization (M ≠ 0 when h = 0). Coercive field (h needed to flip M).
