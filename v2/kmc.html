KMC PORTAL · 2D KINETIC MONTE CARLO
SiGe Thin Film Simulator
Faithful browser implementation of a 2D Kinetic Monte Carlo simulation for Silicon-Germanium thin film etching. Replicates the FORTRAN90 physics: desorption, surface diffusion, and bulk diffusion with Metropolis energy criterion on a full-scale lattice (1000×5000). Computation runs in a Web Worker — the UI stays responsive.

CARD 01
How does temperature affect surface roughness?
Question: Does a hotter substrate produce a rougher or smoother film?
Method: Temperature sweep 300→1000K, 8 points. Look for: Relationship between T and final roughness (RMS).
CARD 02
What is the growth universality class?
Question: What is the roughness exponent β?
Method: Long single run (5000+ iterations), use the fit range controls to select the growth regime, read β from the log-log fit. Look for: Compare β to KPZ (0.333), EW (0.25), random (0.5). Run at multiple lattice sizes L to measure α and z.
CARD 03
SiGe composition and surface morphology
Question: How does Ge fraction affect surface structure?
Method: θ-sweep from 0 to 1, 6 points. Look for: Different compositions → different roughness.
