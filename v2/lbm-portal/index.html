LBM PORTAL · COMPUTATIONAL FLUID DYNAMICS
Lattice Boltzmann Method
D2Q9 Lattice Boltzmann simulation of incompressible flow past obstacles. Watch vortices form and shed as Reynolds number increases. Explore the transition from laminar to turbulent flow.
Physics: The LBM simulates fluid flow by tracking probability distributions of particle velocities on a lattice. At each timestep: particles stream to neighboring nodes and collide (relax toward equilibrium). From these microscopic rules, macroscopic fluid behavior emerges — including the Navier-Stokes equations. The key parameter is the Reynolds number Re = uL/ν, which determines whether flow is laminar (smooth) or turbulent (vortex shedding).

CARD 01
When do vortices appear?
Question: At what Reynolds number does the flow behind a cylinder become unsteady?
Method: Re-sweep from 1 to 200, 10 points, 3000 steps/point. Look for: Re < 5 → creeping flow. 5 < Re < 47 → steady vortices. Re > 47 → von Kármán street.
CARD 02
How often do vortices shed?
Question: What is the shedding frequency for vortex shedding flow?
Method: Run at Re ≈ 100, extended steps (5000), measure Strouhal number St. Look for: St ≈ 0.2 for a circular cylinder — a universal result.
CARD 03
Circle vs square — which has more drag?
Question: Does obstacle shape affect drag?
Method: Fix Re ≈ 50, run circle then square, compare Cd. Look for: Square has higher Cd due to sharp corners causing earlier separation.
