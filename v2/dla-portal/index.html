DLA PORTAL · FRACTAL GROWTH
Diffusion-Limited Aggregation
Random walkers diffuse and stick to a growing cluster, producing beautiful fractal structures. The resulting aggregate has fractal dimension D ≈ 1.71, emerging purely from the dynamics of diffusion.
Physics: DLA models growth processes limited by diffusion: electrodeposition, mineral dendrites, lightning patterns, bacterial colonies. Particles launched from afar random-walk until they touch the cluster and stick. The outer tips grow fastest because they intercept walkers first, producing branching fractal structures.

CARD 01
What is the fractal dimension of DLA?
Question: Is a DLA aggregate a line (D=1), a filled circle (D=2), or something in between?
Method: Grow 10000 particles, sticking prob = 1.0, point seed. Read D from M(r) plot. Look for: D ≈ 1.71. The aggregate is fractal.
CARD 02
Does stickiness change the fractal?
Question: If particles don’t always stick on first contact, does the aggregate look different?
Method: Sweep sticking probability from 0.05 to 1.0 (8 points), 5000 particles each. Look for: Low sticking → denser, higher D. High sticking → branchy, lower D.
CARD 03
How does the seed shape matter?
Question: Does the initial seed geometry change the growth pattern?
Method: Grow 5000 particles with point seed, line seed, and circle seed. Compare D. Look for: Different seed shapes may produce different fractal dimensions and aggregate morphologies.
