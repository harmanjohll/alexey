PERCOLATION PORTAL · CRITICAL PHENOMENA
2D Site Percolation
Occupy sites on a square lattice with probability p. At a critical threshold pc ≈ 0.5927, a connected cluster spanning the entire lattice suddenly appears. Explore this geometric phase transition.
Physics: Percolation is one of the simplest models showing a phase transition. Below pc, only small isolated clusters exist. At pc, a fractal spanning cluster emerges with dimension D ≈ 91/48 ≈ 1.896. Above pc, most occupied sites belong to one giant connected component.

CARD 01
When does a path appear?
Question: At what occupation probability does a connected path first span the lattice?
Method: Sweep p from 0.3 to 0.8 (25 points), L=100, 10 realizations/point. Look for: Spanning probability jumps from ~0 to ~1 near p ≈ 0.593.
CARD 02
Does lattice size sharpen the transition?
Question: How does the percolation transition change with system size?
Method: Run p-sweeps at L = 20, 50, 100, 200, 500. Look for: Curves get steeper with larger L, all cross near pc.
CARD 03
What does the critical cluster look like?
Question: At pc, is the spanning cluster space-filling or fractal?
Method: Generate at p=0.593, L=200, measure fractal dimension via box-counting. Look for: D ≈ 91/48 ≈ 1.896.
