The Double Pendulum.

what does this mean?
This simulation uses Runge-Kutta 4 integration — a highly accurate method for solving differential equations. The double pendulum equations come from Lagrangian mechanics. The chaotic behavior is real physics, not a simulation artifact.
The Lyapunov exponent measures how fast nearby trajectories diverge. A positive Lyapunov exponent is the mathematical signature of chaos — it means tiny differences grow exponentially. This is why long-term prediction of the double pendulum is impossible, even though the physics is completely deterministic.
Energy conservation is monitored to verify simulation quality. Small drift (<1%) confirms the RK4 integrator is working accurately.
